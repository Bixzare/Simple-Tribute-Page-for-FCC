The Father of Linux and the Architect of Open Source

Linus Torvalds at a TED talk

Here's a tineline of Linus Torvalds' life

  • 1969 - Born in Helsinki, Finland December 28, 1969: Linus Benedict Torvalds is born to Nils and Anna Torvalds, both journalists. His grandfather, Ole Torvalds, was a statistician and poet.
  • 1981 - Early Interest in Computers At the age of 11, Linus begins programming on his grandfather’s Commodore VIC-20, sparking his passion for computers.
  • 1988 - Enrolls at the University of Helsinki Begins studying computer science and discovers Unix, an operating system that will later inspire his own work.
  • 1991 - Creates Linux April: Purchases his first PC, an Intel 80386-based machine, and starts working on a free operating system kernel as a hobby. August 25: Announces his project on a Usenet group, sharing the first version of the Linux kernel (version 0.01) with the world. October 5: Releases Linux version 0.02, marking the official birth of the Linux operating system.
  • 1996 - Completes Master’s Thesis Defends his thesis, titled "Linux: A Portable Operating System," at the University of Helsinki. Linux gains widespread adoption, becoming a cornerstone of the open-source movement.
  • 1997 - Moves to the United States Joins Transmeta Corporation, a startup developing energy-efficient microprocessors, while continuing to oversee Linux development.
  • 2003 - Creates Git Frustrated with existing version control systems, Linus develops Git, a distributed version control system that revolutionizes software development.
  • 2005 - Recognized for His Contributions Named one of Time Magazine’s "100 Most Influential People in the World." Receives the Millennium Technology Prize for his contributions to open-source software.
  • 2010 - Becomes a Linux Foundation Fellow Joins the Linux Foundation, allowing him to focus full-time on Linux development.
  • 2012 - Inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame Recognized for his contributions to the development of the Internet and open-source software.
  • 2018 - Microsoft Embraces Linux Microsoft announces it will include a Linux kernel in its Windows operating system, highlighting Linux’s global influence.
  • 2021 - Continues Leading Linux Development Oversees Linux kernel development, which powers millions of devices worldwide, from smartphones to supercomputers.
